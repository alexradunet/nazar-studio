BALAUR

Your personal wise companion that grows with you.

// WHAT IS IT

Most personal AI is rented memory in someone else's cloud. Balaur is the opposite: a wise companion whose source of truth is a vault you own.

It runs host-native on the box as a Bun/Ink CLI named balaur, with a Johnny Decimal Markdown vault, Markdown skills, and one continuous life conversation. No containers, no MCP.

Knowledge splits cleanly onto two rails. Facts and notes are Johnny Decimal Markdown vault entries, indexed by a disposable SQLite FTS5 accelerator. Procedures are Markdown skills in skills/ or vault entries marked kind: skill, invocable with /skill:name.

It's named for the balaur — the multi-headed dragon of Romanian fairy tales. Yours is one wise companion with one master conversation, plus focused sub-heads for scoped branches.

// THE STATEMENT

Your life is not a product.

The record of your life — what you ate, where you walked, what you felt, who you love — should answer to you alone.

  • Your data lives on hardware you control — never as the price of admission to your own memory.
  • Open formats only. Plain files you can read, move, and delete. No hostage-taking.
  • Open standards over private APIs; open source over closed clouds.
  • Private by default — your personal context never trains anyone's model.
  • European by conviction, not by compliance — your ownership as the starting point.
  • Free at the core, forever. You should never have to buy back your own autonomy.

// HOW IT'S BUILT

Build the smallest thing that's truly yours.

Balaur is shaped by a few hard-won principles: local-first by default, files over platforms, and growth by explicit approval. Pareto, KISS, YAGNI, made operational.

Files over platforms

Facts and procedures are plain Markdown in a repo and a vault you own — versioned in git, never an opaque memory service.

Two rails, one ethos

Facts ride the Johnny Decimal vault; procedures ride Markdown skills. Each on the simplest rail that fits.

Disposable index

Markdown remains the source of truth; SQLite FTS5 is an accelerator that can be rebuilt.

Branches that return

Focused sub-conversations can compact and merge back into the master life thread.

Suggest → approve

The agent proposes new skills or consolidations. The human approves. Git records the change. No silent self-modification.

Woven, not rendered

A storybook, hand-built look in tactile pixel-art. Craftsmanship you can see is craftsmanship you can trust.

// LIVE NOW

Each focused scope is a head.

“When Balaur branches, a sub-head takes one scope. When the work is done, it closes and merges back into the main head.”

Balaur ships as a standalone CLI. Vault tools — vault_write, vault_search, vault_get, vault_duplicates — work over the FTS5-backed Markdown vault. Skills are Markdown files or vault entries with kind: skill. The approval/review loop is deliberately human-owned.
